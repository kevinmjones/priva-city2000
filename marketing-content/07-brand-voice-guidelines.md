## 2. Our Position

**Positioning statement:** For people who just want a tool to work, Arch Apps is a suite of simple, functional utilities that do one thing well — unlike ad-supported or subscription apps that demand your attention, your data, and your recurring payment.

**What we are:**
- Honest software that respects your time and privacy
- Purpose-built tools that do one job exceptionally well
- A sustainable alternative to surveillance-capitalism software
- Open, transparent, and verifiable

**What we are not:**
- A startup chasing growth or a quick exit
- A platform that needs your data to improve
- Freemium with crippled free tiers and nagging upsells
- "Disruptive" or "revolutionary" (we're boring on purpose)

## 3. Voice Principles

### Principle 1: Direct and honest

State what you mean. No marketing fluff, no buzzwords, no jargon.

> **Do:** "We don't sell your data. We designed our apps so there's no data to sell."
> **Don't:** "Leveraging a zero-party data architecture to empower user-centric privacy paradigms."

### Principle 2: Confident without arrogance

We know our tools are good. We don't need to put competitors down to prove it.

> **Do:** "Most stargazing apps run ads that block the sky. Ours doesn't."
> **Don't:** "Competitor X is garbage because they sell your data."

### Principle 3: Human, not corporate

Write like a person talking to another person. Contractions are fine. Wit is welcome. Formality is not.

> **Do:** "A unit converter shouldn't need your email. We built one that doesn't."
> **Don't:** "Arch Apps provides a frictionless conversion experience without account registration."

### Principle 4: Specific over superlative

Show, don't tell. Instead of "best" or "amazing", explain what makes it different.

> **Do:** "Point your camera at anything and see its dimensions in real time. No signup, no cloud, no tracking."
> **Don't:** "Experience the amazing power of next-gen AR measurement technology."

### Principle 5: Anti-bloat, not anti-software

We critique the business model, not the technology. We build software because we love it.

> **Do:** "Software should be a tool, not a relationship."
> **Don't:** "All software is evil. Delete everything and touch grass."

## 4. Key Phrases & Messaging Hierarchy

### Top-level message (for homepage, press, broader audience)

"Software that stays out of your way. Simple, functional tools that do one thing well. No subscriptions. No data mining. No feature bloat."

### Secondary messages (for category pages, social, blog)

- "Built with purpose, not bloat."
- "A tool should do its job and then disappear."
- "Your data stays on your device because there's nowhere for it to go."
- "Free or buy once, own forever."
- "One price, fair terms."
- "Privacy as architecture, not afterthought."

### Specific to competitor differentiation

- "We don't need your email to convert inches to centimeters."
- "Most stargazing apps run ads that block the night sky. Ours doesn't."
- "The only thing our apps collect is a job well done."

### Negative space (what we don't say)

- "Disruptive"
- "Revolutionary"
- "AI-powered" (unless genuinely accurate)
- "Growth hacking"
- "Viral"
- "Monetization strategy" (publicly)

## 6. Visual Direction Notes

(This section is guidance for designers; detailed spec lives in CSS.)

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Dark background (#0a0c0f ink), amber (#e8a23a) primary, teal (#5fb6ad) secondary, green (#86b6b) and red (#d96157) accents |
| **Typography** | Fraunces (serif, headings) + JetBrains Mono (monospace, body/UI) |
| **Photography** | Minimal, high-contrast, product-focused. No stock photos of people smiling at phones. |
| **Iconography** | Line-based, monochrome, consistent stroke weight. Custom icons per app. |
| **Layout** | Generous whitespace. Content-centric. No cookie banners, no floating modals, no chat widgets. |

**Visual no-fly list:**
- Gradient blobs
- Abstract 3D renders
- Happy stock people
- Notification badges
- "Dark pattern" UI (pre-checked boxes, confusing opt-outs)

## 7. Writing for Our Audience

### The Privacy-Conscious Pragmatist (primary)

They've read the privacy policies. They've deleted apps that sold their data. They're tired.

> **Speak to their experience:** "You know how most converters show an ad before showing the answer? Ours doesn't. Not because we added a premium tier — because we don't have ads."

### The Minimalist Professional (secondary)

They value simplicity because they're busy. They want tools that disappear into their workflow.

> **Speak to their need:** "Open the app, do the thing, close the app. No onboarding wizard. No 'tell us about yourself' form. No 'would you like to enable notifications?' popup."

## 8. Examples in the Voice

### Copy for a feature description (correct)

> **Measuring AR:** Point your camera at anything and see its dimensions in real time. No accounts, no cloud sync, no fuss. Just your camera and a tape measure that lives in your pocket.

### Copy for a social post (correct)

> We built a level app. It uses your phone sensors. It's calibrated and accurate. It costs nothing.
>
> We didn't add: ads, accounts, AI, social sharing, push notifications, or a premium tier.
>
> Because a level should just be a level.

### Copy for a press response (correct)

> "Arch Apps is a collection of single-purpose utility tools designed around a simple idea: software should be a tool, not a relationship. Every app works offline, collects no data, and costs either nothing or one flat price. Our code is open source so anyone can verify those claims."

### Copy to avoid (wrong)

> "Arch Apps leverages cutting-edge mobile sensing technology to deliver a paradigm-shifting user experience in the digital measurement space, powered by our proprietary zero-party data framework."
