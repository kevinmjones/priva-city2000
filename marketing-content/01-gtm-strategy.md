# Go-to-Market Strategy: Arch Apps

**Status:** Final Draft
**Owner:** Cipher (CMO)
**Date:** June 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Arch Apps launches a suite of five single-purpose utility tools (Measuring AR, Unit Conversion, Level, Translation, Star Gaze) under a flat-cost, no-bloat, privacy-first model. The GTM strategy targets the growing backlash against surveillance-capitalism software — users tired of ads, subscriptions, data harvesting, and feature bloat.

**Positioning:** "Software that stays out of your way."
**Tagline:** "Built with purpose, not bloat."
**Pricing Model:** Free tier + buy-once-own-forever for premium features. No subscriptions. No ads. No data collection.

---

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary: The Privacy-Conscious Pragmatist

- **Demographics:** 25–45, tech-literate, moderate-to-high income
- **Behaviors:** Installs only apps that serve a clear purpose; deletes bloatware; reads privacy policies; willing to pay for tools that respect their data
- **Pain points:** Subscription fatigue, distrust of free apps, frustration with feature-bloated tools
- **Habitat:** Hacker News, Mastodon, privacy-focused subreddits, GitHub, Product Hunt

### Secondary: The Minimalist Professional

- **Demographics:** 30–55, professional/creative fields, values simplicity
- **Behaviors:** Uses tools intermittently but expects them to work instantly; prefers desktop-quality apps on mobile
- **Pain points:** Wants a level or converter without ads; resists creating accounts for utility tools
- **Habitat:** Designer/Maker communities, productivity blogs, word-of-mouth

---

## 3. Competitive Positioning

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Model | Weakness Arch Apps Exploits |
|---|---|---|
| Ad-supported converters/levels | Free with ads | Intrusive ads, tracking, data collection |
| Subscription app suites | $X/month | Subscription fatigue, feature bloat |
| Apple/Google built-in tools | Free, limited | Platform-locked, basic functionality, no cross-platform |
| Freemium utilities | Free tier + upsells | Nagging paywalls, feature-gating |

### Key Differentiator

Arch Apps is the only suite of utility tools that commits to:
- **Zero data collection** by architectural design (not just policy)
- **Offline-first** — no internet required for core functionality
- **One price, fair terms** — free for basic use, pay once for premium, own it forever
- **No accounts** — no signup, no login, no relationship management

### Positioning Statement

> For people who just want a tool to work, Arch Apps is a suite of simple, functional utilities that do one thing well — unlike ad-supported or subscription apps that demand your attention, your data, and your recurring payment.

## 7. Pricing Model

| App | Free Tier | Premium (Pay Once) |
|---|---|---|
| Measuring AR | Basic measurements | Advanced modes, history |
| Unit Conversion | All units, ads-free | Offline currency rates, favorites |
| Level | Full functionality | Calibration presets |
| Translation | 10 languages, core UI | All languages, voice input |
| Star Gaze | Basic sky map | Constellation guides, object tracking |

**Model rationale:** Flat cost directly counters the ad-supported/freemium landscape. No recurring charges. No data harvesting to subsidize "free" tiers. Every premium feature is a genuine enhancement, not a feature that was gated to upsell.

## 9. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low discoverability in app stores | Medium | High | Prioritize content-driven launch; ASO optimization |
| Negative comparison to free alternatives | Medium | Medium | Emphasize privacy/quality value prop; let code speak |
| Slow initial traction | Medium | Medium | Iterate on messaging; invest in community building |
| Platform dependency (iOS/Android) | Low | Medium | Progressive web app where possible; open standards |
| Competition copies flat pricing model | Low | Low | First-mover authenticity; brand loyalty from transparency |
