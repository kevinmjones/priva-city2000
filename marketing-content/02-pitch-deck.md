## Slide 1: Title

**Arch Apps**  
Software that stays out of your way.  
Simple, functional tools. No bloat. No subscriptions. No data mining.

---

## Slide 2: The Problem

**Software stopped being a tool.**

- The average utility app demands: your email, your location, your contacts, your data — just to convert inches to centimeters
- Subscription fatigue: 45% of consumers say they resent paying monthly for software they use twice a year
- Ad-supported utilities track, profile, and sell user data to stay "free"
- Feature bloat: apps that started as single-purpose tools now ship with chat, AI assistants, and social feeds

**The industry optimized for everyone except the person using the product.**

---

## Slide 3: The Solution

**Arch Apps — simple tools that do one thing well.**

- Five single-purpose utilities: Measuring AR, Unit Conversion, Level, Translation, Star Gaze
- Offline by default — no internet required, no data collected
- Every feature earns its place by making the tool better at its primary job
- Built in the open — public code on GitHub, transparent process

"Our tagline: Built with purpose, not bloat."

---

## Slide 4: Why Now

**The market is ready for an alternative.**

| Trend | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Privacy backlash | 82% of users say privacy matters more than 3 years ago (Pew, 2025) |
| Subscription fatigue | 58% of consumers say they have too many subscriptions |
| "Digital decluttering" movement | Growing search interest in minimal tech, dumb phones, offline tools |
| Open-source credibility | Developers and power users influence purchasing; they trust open code |

- Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA) make data-heavy models costly
- Flat-cost tools are a proven model (Panic, Sublime Text, BBEdit)

---

## Slide 6: Business Model

**Flat cost. Fair terms. Sustainable.**

| Tier | Price | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Full basic functionality for all apps |
| Premium (one-time) | $0.99–$4.99 per app | Advanced features, no subscriptions ever |
| Bundle | $9.99 | All 5 apps premium, perpetual license |

**Why this works:**
- No recurring revenue pressure → builds long-term product trust
- Bundle upsell drives higher LTV per user
- Free tier is genuinely useful (not crippled) → builds goodwill and word-of-mouth
- Minimal infrastructure costs (offline-first) → high margin per paid user
