### Full Text

I built Arch Apps — five single-purpose utility tools on a simple premise: software should be a tool, not a relationship.

The five apps:
- Measuring AR (point camera → real-time dimensions)
- Unit Conversion (offline, instant, 100+ categories)
- Level (precision spirit level from device sensors)
- Translation (on-device, your words never leave your phone)
- Star Gaze (night sky identification, zero ads)

What makes them different:
- **Zero data collection** — built architecturally, not just a privacy policy. No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry.
- **Offline by default** — no internet required for core functionality
- **No accounts** — no signup, no login, no "relationship management"
- **Pay once, own forever** — free for basic use. Premium features are a one-time purchase. No subscriptions.

I built this because I'm tired of opening a unit converter and being asked for my email, my location, and a monthly payment. A hammer doesn't ask for your email. A tape measure doesn't sell your data. Software tools shouldn't either.

Everything is open source: [github.com/kevinmjones/priva-city2000](https://github.com/kevinmjones/priva-city2000)

Website: [archapps.dev](https://archapps.dev)

I'd love your honest feedback — on the apps, the approach, the philosophy. What resonates? What doesn't? What utility would make you actually switch from your current tool?

## Comment Preparation

### Expected questions and prepared responses:

**Q: "Why not just use the built-in level / converter on my phone?"**
> Fair question. The built-in tools are fine for basic use but limited. Level adds calibration presets and angle readouts. Unit Conversion handles 100+ categories. But the real answer is: these exist so we don't have to settle for tools that come bundled with an OS and a data collection policy we can't opt out of.

**Q: "How do you make money?"**
> Premium features are a one-time purchase per app. No subscriptions. The model only works if the apps are genuinely useful — which keeps us honest. We're not trying to extract recurring revenue from a level.

**Q: "Why open source?"**
> If you're asking users to trust that you don't collect data, the burden of proof is on you. Open source means anyone can verify the claim. We have nothing to hide.

**Q: "Are these PWAs or native?"**
> [Coordinate with CTO on technical answer]

**Q: "Why these five apps?"**
> We started with the tools we use most often and hate the available options for. Each one solves a specific frustration with the incumbent apps — ads on a level, data collection on a converter, subscriptions on a stargazing app.
