Our roadmap

Single-purpose, privacy-first, ad-free — now in four waves. Six apps live, the rest planned or in evaluation. Wave 4 adds apps that each replace a documented data-harvesting incumbent. Here's what's live and what's coming next.

01 Now — Wave 1 (Q3 2026) 4-6 weeks
Utility iOS / Android / Web

QR & Barcode Scanner ✓

Point, scan, done. No ads, no tracking, no permissions beyond the camera. Your scans stay on your device — a unique claim in a category where most scanners send data to ad networks.

  • QR code decoding (URL, text, WiFi, vCard)
  • UPC/EAN/Code128 barcode support
  • On-device scan history (100-entry LRU)
  • Zero network permission — full offline

04 Candidates — Wave 4 (privacy by replacement) under evaluation

A candidate set where each app exists to replace a documented data-harvesting incumbent. Every entry names the category it replaces, what that category takes, who gets it, and what it is used for — drawn from FTC actions and investigative reporting. Priority: ship Flashlight + Photo Metadata Scrubber first (quick wins), then Cycle Tracker as the flagship narrative app.

Utility iOS / Android

Flashlight

The most famous privacy-abuse app in history was a flashlight. Ours is the honest twin: zero permissions, no networking code at all.

Replaces Brightest Flashlight (FTC, 2013) — sent precise location + device ID to ad networks the instant it opened, even if you tapped "Refuse," for ad targeting and resale.
  • Zero permissions, zero network code
  • Instant on, full-brightness toggle, screen-light fallback
  • Nothing to collect, nothing to send
Privacy iOS / Android

Photo Metadata Scrubber

One tap removes the GPS coordinates and EXIF metadata baked into your photos — on-device, before you ever share them.

Replaces the location-leak surface fed by broker SDKs (FTC v. X-Mode, 2024) — GPS/EXIF goes to brokers like Gravy and Kochava (~1B devices) for re-identification and profiling segments.
  • Strip GPS + EXIF/IPTC in one tap, fully on-device
  • Batch-clean before sharing or exporting
  • No upload, no tracking, no account
Health iOS / Android

Cycle Tracker — flagship

A 100%-on-device period and fertility tracker. No account, no cloud, no third-party SDK — nothing on a server to subpoena.

Replaces Flo & Premom (FTC, 2021 & 2023) — cycle dates, symptoms, and the fact of a pregnancy went to Facebook, Google, AppsFlyer and analytics SDKs for ad targeting; a 2025 jury found Meta liable under California's wiretap law.
  • On-device cycle, symptom, and prediction logging
  • No account, no cloud sync, no third-party SDK
  • Optional on-device passcode / encryption
Utility iOS / Android

Local Weather

A forecast from a coarse, on-device-resolved location against a public weather API — no profile, no resale, no hedge-fund pipe.

Replaces The Weather Channel / IBM (LA City Attorney, 2019) — collected precise location under a "personalized forecast" prompt and sold it to ad sites and hedge funds.
  • Coarse location resolved on-device
  • Public weather API, no location resale
  • No advertising profile built from where you are
Utility iOS / Android

Doc & Receipt Scanner

Capture documents and receipts to clean PDFs on-device, metadata stripped automatically. The only egress is the export you choose. Pairs with QR Scanner and Expense Tracker.

Replaces cloud scanner apps (ad-laden, with a malware history) — upload images of your documents and IDs "to process," exposing them to breach, subpoena, and model training.
  • On-device capture, edge detection, PDF export
  • Automatic EXIF/metadata stripping
  • Export is the only path data leaves the device
Productivity iOS / Android

Secure Notes

A plaintext/markdown notepad that is local-only with optional on-device encryption. No account, no sync, no "your notes, our servers."

Replaces account/cloud-gated note apps (Mozilla found ~80% of Play "Data Safety" labels misleading) — keep a permanent copy of your private writing on infrastructure you don't control.
  • Local-only plaintext / markdown notes
  • Optional on-device encryption
  • No account, no sync, no data center
Stretch needs E2EE server

Nearby / Family Locator — held

A privacy-respecting family location-share — the explicit anti-Life360 — using end-to-end encryption and no broker resale. Held deliberately: it needs a server and breaks our pure-offline model, so it requires an explicit architecture call (E2EE relay, zero-knowledge) before we commit.

Replaces Life360 (The Markup, 2021) — sold continuous precise GPS from ~33M users to data brokers including X-Mode, Cuebiq, and Arity.
